Case Study

Distracted Pedestrians: Phone Walking Study

Examining how routine phone use while walking affects urban attention, movement behavior, and social interaction in shared public spaces.

Overview

This study explores phone walking as an everyday behavior, focusing on how pedestrians balance safety, social expectations, and digital engagement while navigating city environments.

PDF of Full Study

Open Distracted Pedestrians Study (PDF)

Key Insights

  • Phone walking is habitual and socially normalized.
  • Pedestrians perform risk-calculated attentional regulation.
  • Mobile engagement redistributes cognitive and physical load onto surrounding pedestrians.
  • Phone presence alone can provide comfort or buffer loneliness.

Method

Hybrid ethnography: field observations and contextual inquiry at three urban locations (Chicago, San Francisco, Minneapolis).
