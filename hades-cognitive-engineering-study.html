UX Research Case Study

The Science of the Underworld

A deep-dive into attentional zone strategy and decision architecture, using game heuristics as a rigorous UX research method.

Study Focus

This case is a process showcase. Instead of judging surface visuals, I mapped how cognition behaves under speed, uncertainty, and threat. Cognitive engineering is the differentiator: decisions are evaluated by mental workload, response timing, and signal prioritization.

Method Breakdown

  1. Define attentional zones: Separate persistent signals from high-volatility combat signals.
  2. Analyze Fresh File constraints: Evaluate decisions when build certainty is minimal and cognitive pressure is high.
  3. Compare expert heuristics: Track why stable options (e.g., defensive mobility choices) reduce working-memory burden.
  4. Translate to UX practice: Convert findings into principles for high-flow/high-stress professional interfaces.

Application to Professional Interfaces

Gaming heuristics reveal how people filter noise when milliseconds matter. I apply this to dashboards, command centers, and trading-style interfaces where users must maintain flow while managing rapidly changing information density.

Why this matters

Shows scientific rigor in decision-making. My design recommendations are grounded in cognitive models, not aesthetics alone, which builds trust with product, engineering, and leadership stakeholders.
