About

Hi, I'm Erin

I'm a UX researcher and designer based in the San Francisco Bay Area, with a Master's in Human-Computer Interaction and a background in cognitive science and psychology.

Accessibility has been part of how I think about design from the beginning. I see it as a lens for building things that genuinely work for people across a wide range of contexts, abilities, and needs.

I work across research, design, and systems thinking. What draws me to this field is the problem-solving: figuring out what's actually happening, why, and building something better as a result.

Right now I'm especially focused on how people make sense of, trust, and collaborate with AI systems, and what that means for the people designing them.
