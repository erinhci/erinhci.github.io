AI Transparency & User Trust

Perceived Transparency in Generative AI

A qualitative study on how users evaluate ChatGPT credibility during complex explanatory tasks.

Project Impact

The research reframes AI transparency as a conversational outcome, not a static interface feature, identifying practical design actions that improve trust calibration before high-stakes use cases.

Goal & Study Design

The goal was to understand how users evaluate ChatGPT’s credibility during complex explanatory tasks, including science concepts such as why the moon appears to change size over time.

Methodology: Qualitative study (n=4) using 5-minute live contextual inquiry sessions, followed by semi-structured interviews and thematic analysis.

Process

Contextual Inquiry

Observed live explanatory sessions to capture trust judgments as they formed in real time.

Think-Aloud Protocol

Prompted participants to narrate why specific response patterns increased or reduced credibility.

Thematic Analysis

Coded interview and session data into recurrent trust heuristics and transparency behaviors.

Key Findings

  1. Heuristic Cues over Citations: Participants rarely searched for external references. They trusted responses that sounded professional, used clear step-by-step structure, and maintained logical flow.
  2. Co-Constructed Transparency: Transparency emerged when users could ask follow-up questions and probe the model’s logic, rather than from one final answer alone.
  3. Over-Reliance Risk: Confident, polished language encouraged misplaced trust in low-stakes tasks—a pattern that could become dangerous in medical or legal contexts.

The “Moon Phase” Example

One participant began with confusion about whether the moon physically changes size. Through iterative prompting, they asked the model to restate the answer, break down orbital position, and explain apparent size changes from Earth’s perspective.

Trust increased only after this back-and-forth clarified that lunar phases are about illumination angle and orbital geometry, not the moon expanding or shrinking. The interaction showed that understanding was built through dialogue, not delivered in a single response.

Design Recommendations

  • Optimize for structural clarity: Prioritize progressive, well-signposted explanations.
  • Encourage conversational probing: Prompt users with follow-up paths that test assumptions.
  • Balance polish with uncertainty signals: Surface confidence limits when claims may be incomplete.
