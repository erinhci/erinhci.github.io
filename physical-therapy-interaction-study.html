Physical Therapy Interaction Study

Measuring Team Efficiency

A cognitive engineering study of communication bottlenecks and workflow reliability in clinical operations.

The Objective

This study evaluated how communication among physical therapists, assistants, and receptionists impacts throughput, care continuity, and clinic workflow reliability across high-traffic treatment periods.

Methodology

  • Contextual Inquiry: shadowed live interactions to map real-time collaboration decisions.
  • Event Logging: captured workflow disruptions and handoff latency during clinic peaks.
  • Thematic Analysis: synthesized recurring communication patterns with OSI markers and HDP model.

Insights & Findings

Communication efficiency and team “vibe” emerged as core performance metrics. Phone-based scheduling often interrupted active patient care and fragmented treatment continuity.
